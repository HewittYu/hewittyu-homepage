About

I am an incoming PhD student in the School of Electronics and Computer Science at the University of Southampton. My research focuses on Medical AI, with particular interests in digital pathology, human-in-the-loop learning, and multimodal methods for clinically useful screening and decision support.

My recent work has centred on high-recall segmentation and screening systems designed to minimise missed lesions while maintaining practical utility. I am especially interested in reliable model design, uncertainty-aware workflows, expert-guided refinement, and the integration of image evidence with structured or weakly supervised signals.

More broadly, I work on deep learning for medical image analysis, with emphasis on robustness, interpretability, and deployment-aware evaluation.

Featured Projects

ClinSegNet — High-Recall Histopathology Screening System
A human-centric segmentation framework for histopathology screening, designed to prioritise recall and reduce missed lesions. The system combines organ-aware conditioning, SE-enhanced U-Net design, edge-aware supervision, and practical evaluation for reliable pre-diagnostic support.
Paper
HMSA-SegNet — Parameter-Efficient Medical Image Segmentation
A lightweight segmentation architecture integrating hierarchical multi-scale attention, adaptive multi-path feature extraction, and transformer-based interaction, achieving strong performance with a compact parameter budget.
Multi-modal Liver Image Segmentation on CHAOS
Undergraduate thesis work on multi-modal liver image segmentation, exploring modified U-Net variants, attention mechanisms, and multimodal fusion strategies for improved robustness.
